one girl said it was so natural, it was like I was just playing records for myself on a sunday afternoon. the older fellow said that he once had a girlfriend from chicago, but it was a long long time ago, and that the Map.ache song almost made him cry. I think it was him that said this party was a dose of freshness for a small scene. I also remember one fellow remarking that I handled records in a strange way, very carefully and not queueing on the recorded surface. He said he liked that and had never seen it before. There was one girl who was dressed a bit like a hippy and said after the set that it was very unprofessional of me to wear earplugs in the club, said that I wasn't doing my job and wanted me to take them out. The bartender had awesome purple pants with diagonal zippers. I gathered a bit of field recordings from the car on the drive back to dugave with mirta, nils, and the driver (same fellow who came back to dugave with us after masters, kind of a stern fellow.)
